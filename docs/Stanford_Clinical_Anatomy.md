## 1. Advanced Search Query Framework (`site:gov`, `site:edu`, `site:mil`)

To verify anatomical dimensions, tissue tensile strengths, or physiological parameters, use the following standardized search string matrices across authenticated federal, educational, and defense repositories.

### A. Vascular Modeling & Non-Newtonian Rheology
*   **Target Metrics**: Murray's Law deviations, Fåhræus-Lindqvist limits, and arterial wall tensile constraints.
*   **Search Syntax**:
    ```text
    "Fahræus-Lindqvist effect" AND "microvascular wall shear" AND "ultimate tensile strength" site:gov OR site:edu
    ```
    ```text
    "Murray's law" AND "vascular tree generation" AND "fractal scaling" site:ncbi.nlm.nih.gov OR site:edu
    ```

### B. Intracranial Dynamics & Glymphatic Shunts
*   **Target Metrics**: Intracranial pressure (ICP) compliance gradients, cerebrospinal fluid (CSF) flow velocities, and cribriform plate perivascular porous dimensions.
*   **Search Syntax**:

    ```text
    "glymphatic clearance" AND "dural venous sinuses" AND "intracranial pressure" site:ncbi.nlm.nih.gov
    ```

### C. Splenic Engorgement & Portal Vein Hypertension
*   **Target Metrics**: Splenic tissue compliance, capsular rupture volume parameters, and interendothelial slit size-exclusion filtration ceilings.
*   **Search Syntax**:

## 2. Core Library Data Sinks (Authenticated Registries)

When executing continuous pipeline validations or automating patient profile boundaries, look up primary data vectors from the following institutional databases:

1.  **National Center for Biotechnology Information (NCBI) PubMed Central** (`ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc`)
    *   *Usage*: Verification of Michaelis-Menten macrophage clearance rates, troponin-C binding constants, and fibrinolysis equations.
2.  **Defense Technical Information Center (DTIC)** (`discover.dtic.mil`)
    *   *Usage*: Sourcing high-energy impact tissue rupture limits, systemic vascular trauma kinetics, and emergency fluid resuscitation models.
3.  **Visible Human Project / National Library of Medicine (NLM)** (`nlm.nih.gov/research/visible`)
    *   *Usage*: Direct 3D spatial coordinate voxel generation validation, cross-sectional organ partitioning metrics, and absolute physical lengths of gastrointestinal and urogenital segments.
